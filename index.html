Frequently asked questions

Maths Revision — Common Questions

What is an A-level Maths revision course?

An A-level Maths revision course is an intensive online programme designed to consolidate syllabus understanding and build exam technique ahead of A-level examinations. Dr Korte's courses cover Pure Mathematics, Statistics and Mechanics in small groups, with targeted support for Edexcel, AQA, OCR A and OCR B students.

Do you offer Further Maths tuition?

Yes. Courses cover both A-level Mathematics and A-level Further Mathematics. Further Maths tuition covers the additional pure mathematics, statistics, mechanics and decision mathematics content required for the Further Maths A-level, and is available as part of the same small group online programme.

Which exam boards do you cover for Maths?

Courses cover all major UK A-level Mathematics exam boards: Edexcel, AQA, OCR A and OCR B.

How does online Maths tuition work?

Sessions run live via Zoom in small groups. Dr Korte teaches using shared digital materials, works through problems in real time, and tailors explanations to the questions that arise. Students need only a device with internet access.

When are the Easter and summer Maths revision courses?

Easter intensive courses run during the Easter school holiday period in April — ideal for final exam preparation before the summer exams. Summer intensive courses run in August, ahead of resit examinations or Year 12 progression. Christmas intensive courses run between Christmas and New Year, giving Year 12 students a head start on their second term and Year 13 students early exam preparation. Exact dates and booking are at physicsandmathscourses.com.

Is the course suitable for Year 12 and Year 13?

Yes. Courses are available for both Year 12 students consolidating their first year of A-level Maths and Year 13 students preparing for final examinations. Course content is tailored to the specific year group and exam board of each cohort.
