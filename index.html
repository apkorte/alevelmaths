Maths Revision — Common Questions

An A-level Maths revision course is an intensive online programme designed to consolidate syllabus understanding and build exam technique ahead of A-level examinations. Dr Korte's courses cover Pure Mathematics (algebra, coordinate geometry, calculus, trigonometry, sequences and series), Statistics, and Mechanics in small groups via Zoom, with targeted support for Edexcel, AQA, OCR A and OCR B students. Each session works through past-paper style questions in real time, with Dr Korte explaining methods, identifying common errors, and adapting to the questions that arise. The Easter intensive is particularly popular for Year 13 students preparing for summer exams, while the Christmas and summer courses suit both year groups. Course fees range from £95 for a 1-day session to £295 for the 4-day Easter course.

Yes. Courses cover both A-level Mathematics and A-level Further Mathematics. Further Maths tuition covers the additional pure mathematics, statistics, mechanics and decision mathematics content required for the Further Maths A-level, and is available as part of the same small group online programme. Dr Korte's MSc in Mathematical Modelling from University College London covers much of the same advanced material — complex numbers, differential equations, matrices, polar coordinates — that appears in Further Maths A-level, giving him an unusually deep command of the subject from first principles. Edexcel, AQA, OCR A and OCR B are all covered. Further Maths students benefit from dedicated sessions tailored to their additional content requirements.

Courses cover all major UK A-level Mathematics exam boards: Edexcel, AQA, OCR A and OCR B. While the core content — Pure Mathematics, Statistics, and Mechanics — is broadly consistent across all four boards, there are differences in question style, formula sheet provision, and the weighting of statistical topics. Dr Korte is familiar with the specific requirements of each board and tailors examples and past-paper practice to the board each student sits. Students should confirm their exam board when booking so that the right past papers and mark schemes can be prioritised during the session.

Sessions run live via Zoom in small groups. Dr Korte teaches using shared digital materials, works through problems in real time, and tailors explanations to the questions that arise. Students need only a device with internet access — no specialist software is required. The online format works particularly well for A-level Maths because problems can be displayed, annotated, and worked through step-by-step on a shared screen. Students are encouraged to ask questions throughout rather than waiting until the end of a topic. Sessions alternate between concept explanation, worked examples, and student practice, giving each student time to attempt problems before the solution is shown.
